Manchester City’s Struggles: A Tactical Breakdown

By Football News Official Analyst

For years, Manchester City have been the gold standard of European football. Their system under Pep Guardiola seemed untouchable—possession dominance, relentless pressing, and a forward line capable of suffocating opponents. Yet this season tells a different story. City look more human, more vulnerable, and—most surprisingly—predictable.

The Predictability Problem

Watching City now, the first thing that stands out is how opponents have adapted. Low-block defenses close central channels and force City wide, leading to an over-reliance on crosses to Haaland. Despite his talent, he is often left isolated.

Injuries and Midfield Imbalance

The absence of Kevin De Bruyne has been damaging. Without his vertical passing, City’s buildup feels slower. Foden and Cherki step in, but lack KDB’s control. Rodri has been overworked, and when pressed, City’s structure collapses.

Defensive Vulnerabilities

Laporte’s departure left Guardiola without a reliable left-footed center-back. Stones’ inconsistency adds pressure, and transitions often expose Walker and Gvardiol. Counterattacking teams now exploit City with surprising ease.

Comparisons to Rivals

Liverpool’s refreshed attack, Arsenal’s defensive solidity, PSG’s pressing intensity, and Barcelona’s youthful energy highlight City’s current stagnation. Once the innovators, City are now being caught up.

What Next for Guardiola?

Guardiola has reinvented his sides before. But if he fails to adapt quickly, City may lose their aura of inevitability. The coming months could define his legacy at the Etihad.

Manchester City remain contenders, but no longer inevitable. Their aura has cracked—and recovery will demand reinvention.

Liverpool: The Isak Effect

Alexander Isak’s arrival has reshaped Slot’s frontline, adding height, composure, and link-up play.

Slot’s 4-2-3-1 sees Isak drop deep, freeing Salah and Ekitike while Wirtz ghosts in behind. Szoboszlai and Mac Allister exploit spaces, and Frimpong overlaps dangerously. Liverpool now look fluid, unpredictable, and harder to defend against. Defensively, they still struggle in transitions, but attack-wise they look reborn.

Arsenal: Mastering the Low Block

Arteta’s side now combines defensive resilience with attacking efficiency, correcting last year’s flaws.

Declan Rice anchors midfield, while Saliba and Gabriel dominate defensively. Gyökeres’ movement creates space for Ødegaard, who dictates attacks. Arsenal can control possession or sit deep, making them genuine title contenders.

PSG: Dominance with Discipline

Enrique has forged a more structured PSG, built on pressing, intensity, and collective balance.
